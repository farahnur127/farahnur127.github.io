Internalising the Ihsan

Prophet Muhammad (SAW) once said that Ihsan is the highest level of worship, where one worships Allah as if they can see Him. To achieve this level, we should imagine that Allah (SWT) is watching us while we pray, so we can concentrate better. Ihsan is the ultimate level of our religion, whereas the lowest is Islam, and the second-lowest is Iman. It is vital to keep in mind that Allah is always watching us, whether we are talking to our parents or preaching to others. Therefore, we should strive to worship Allah to the highest level, which is Ihsan.

Week 2: Online Class
2sd: 2 November 2023

During the second week of usrah, I learned about the Reasons and Sources of Hikmah from 'Ibadah. I reflected on the idea that there is wisdom behind every act of worship that Allah SWT has commanded us to do, such as prayer, fasting, zakat, and Hajj. Each of these acts has a unique wisdom behind it.

Firstly, prayer can help to discipline a person and make their life structured and systematic. Secondly, fasting can purify the human heart, maintain good health, and cure disease, leading to piety. Thirdly, zakat can help the poor and weak and cleanse our wealth so that we can receive the blessings of sustenance from Allah SWT. Lastly, Hajj can help to scrape away someone's selfishness and arrogance while strengthening their soul and tawhid. It can also remind us of death, preventing us from becoming too complacent with worldly life.

Week 7: Forum on 40th Years Journey of IIUM in Disability Inclusion
7th: 22 December 2023 

Forum

The forum taught the importance of not judging a book by its cover and raised awareness about autism society. Some moderators shared their experiences with disabled people, and one moderator shared how overcoming depression and mental health issues affected her career. They stressed the need for support and treatment from psychologists and families. To reach out to struggling individuals, it is crucial to support each other and seek knowledge about their struggles. Approaching them with empathy and compassion, rather than seeking solutions, is essential. Body language, gestures, emotions, and impressions can convey interest in their story. Observing and not judging quickly is crucial. When someone is almost drowning, seek help and don't be ashamed or embarrassed. It's essential to look up to each other and take care of each other.

Mr Adli, a moderator with an autistic child, shared his experiences with his son's different personalities and attitudes, expressing his belief that Allah gave him an autism spectrum son as a blessing. He also discussed the struggles faced by people with mental health issues, such as blindness and impaired vision. He emphasized the importance of open communication and understanding for those with hidden disabilities.

A moderator with mental illnesses advised seeking help when faced with doubts and avoiding societal discrimination. She encouraged individuals to seek support from people who love themselves and understand their uniqueness. She also encouraged people not to judge those facing challenges, especially those with disabilities, as they may need support.

In conclusion, everyone has uniqueness and needs support, and it is crucial to approach those who need help and understand the challenges they face. It is essential to approach individuals with autism spectrum children and their parents without judgment, as they have different thoughts and feelings. By understanding and supporting those with autism, we can help them overcome their challenges and find solace in the company of others.
